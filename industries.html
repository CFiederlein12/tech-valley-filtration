Healthcare & Senior Living

Critical infection control and patient comfort through advanced filtration

Why Healthcare Needs Superior Filtration

Healthcare facilities, hospitals, surgical centers, and senior living communities require the highest standards of air quality. Infection control is paramount, and proper filtration plays a critical role in preventing the spread of airborne pathogens, protecting immunocompromised patients, and maintaining regulatory compliance.

Elderly residents and patients with compromised immune systems are particularly vulnerable to airborne contaminants. As we age, the body's ability to cope with harmful air pollutants decreases, making superior air quality essential for health and recovery.

Our Healthcare Filtration Solutions

  • MERV 14 Filtration: Removes 90% of particles in the 1.0-3.0 micron range, critical for infection control
  • HEPA Systems: 99.97% efficiency at 0.3 microns for isolation rooms and surgical areas
  • UV-C Disinfection: Additional pathogen elimination for enhanced patient protection
  • Molecular Filtration: Odor and VOC removal for patient comfort
  • Senior Living Solutions: Graduated filtration from independent to skilled nursing

Facility Types We Serve

Hospitals and medical centers, surgical centers and operating rooms, nursing homes and skilled nursing facilities, assisted living communities, independent living apartments, rehabilitation centers, and medical office buildings.

Government & Commercial Buildings

Protecting occupants and equipment in office and public spaces

Indoor Air Quality for Productivity

The quality of air in commercial buildings has a significant impact on human health, comfort, and productivity. Poor indoor air quality leads to discomfort, illness, absenteeism, and reduced productivity, making it an important occupational and environmental health issue.

Modern office buildings face increasing IAQ challenges from tighter sealing for energy conservation, synthetic materials, office equipment (printers, copiers, computers), cleaning products, and outdoor air pollution. Common contaminants include allergens, gases, bioaerosols (viruses, bacteria), and fine particles under 2.5 microns (PM2.5).

Commercial Filtration Best Practices

  • MERV 8 Minimum Standard: Required by ASHRAE 62.1 for acceptable indoor air quality
  • MERV 13 Recommended: Best practice for superior occupant health and comfort
  • Equipment Rooms: Enhanced filtration for data centers and server rooms
  • Renovation Protection: Temporary filtration during construction activities
  • Energy Efficiency: Balanced approach to clean air and operating costs

Facilities We Support

Government office buildings, municipal facilities, courthouses and legal buildings, transportation hubs (airports, transit stations), commercial office towers, retail spaces, restaurants and hospitality, and places of public assembly.

Indoor Firing Ranges

Critical lead containment and worker protection systems

Why Filtration is Critical

Indoor firing ranges generate significant lead dust and toxic fumes from bullet discharge. OSHA mandates strict exposure limits (50 micrograms per cubic meter over 8 hours) to protect shooters and employees. Proper filtration is not optional - it's essential for regulatory compliance and occupant safety.

Lead exposure can cause serious health issues including fatigue, headaches, nervous system damage, and reproductive problems. Without proper filtration, lead particles accumulate in shooting stations, downrange areas, and target zones, creating hazardous conditions.

Mandatory Filtration Requirements

  • HEPA Filtration Required: 99.97% efficiency at 0.3 microns for exhaust air
  • MERV 14 Make-Up Air: Protects HVAC equipment and provides clean breathing air
  • Pre-Filtration: MERV 8 and MERV 14 pre-filters extend HEPA life
  • Molecular Filtration: Removes carbon monoxide and nitrogen oxides when recirculating
  • Specialized Framing: HEPA-rated housings to prevent bypass leakage

Compliance & Safety

All systems must meet OSHA standards, EPA regulations, and NIOSH guidelines. Regular filter monitoring, proper disposal procedures (RCRA hazardous waste), and certified installation testing are mandatory.

Post-COVID Air Quality Requirements

The COVID-19 pandemic heightened awareness that indoor air quality is a critical health and safety concern. Proper filtration is now recognized as essential infrastructure for any occupied building.

Facilities requiring enhanced infection control include healthcare settings, schools, offices, transportation hubs, places of assembly, and any high-occupancy space. The combination of filtration, ventilation rates, and air changes per hour (ACH) determines infection risk reduction.

Infection Control Solutions

  • UV-C Integration: Additional pathogen inactivation layer

Cannabis Cultivation Facilities

Specialized climate control and odor management systems

Unique Cultivation Requirements

Cannabis growing facilities require precise environmental control including temperature, humidity, and air quality management. These facilities face challenges with strong odors, high humidity, mold and mildew risks, and strict regulatory compliance for both air quality and odor containment.

Proper HVAC and filtration systems are essential for healthy plant growth, worker safety, regulatory compliance, and preventing neighborhood odor complaints. The combination of particulate filtration and molecular (carbon) filtration is typically required.

Cultivation Filtration Systems

  • MERV 11-13 Particulate: Removes mold spores, pollen, and plant matter
  • Molecular/Carbon Filtration: Mandatory for odor control and VOC removal
  • Humidity Control: Prevents mold growth that affects both plants and filters
  • Negative Pressure: Keeps odors contained within facility
  • Grow Room Isolation: Separate ventilation for different cultivation stages

Regulatory Compliance

Most jurisdictions require odor control systems that prevent off-site detection. State and local regulations often mandate carbon filtration, negative building pressure, and regular air quality monitoring.
